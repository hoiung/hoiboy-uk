Done-with-you, not done-for-you, and definitely not a slide deck. I train your team to use AI properly, on your real work, not toy examples. I focus mainly on Claude Code (my primary tool, the one I bet my consultancy on), and I can also teach AI in general terms at a high level, so your people learn how to get the most out of most models, not just one.

Sessions can be 1:1 or group. It can be a single-day session to get a team moving, or a programme that runs over several weeks working closely with your teams and departments. It is customised to your team's needs and the depth you want to go into. Some of it is generic good practice; most of it depends on what your people actually do day to day and what you are trying to get out of AI.

This is the middle path between the other two services. With a harness or an automation, I build the thing for you. Here, I get your people genuinely capable themselves, so the skill stays in the building after I leave. Like the automation service, this one exists because I asked around to understand what the market wants and needs: plenty of teams want the capability in-house, they just need someone to show them properly, on their own work.

I like to work in smaller groups. I usually start with an initial run-through to share my AI knowledge and get everyone grounded, and then the practical part is exactly what it sounds like: literally sitting and working alongside your team for a week or two, however complex their roles are. I make myself available to each individual as they work through their actual day with AI, giving pointers and getting into the details of their setup and workflow. It is not quite a harness service, but it lets me guide them in that direction: building their own automation and workflow around their real work, rather than a workflow that gets pushed onto them.

## How it works

I run training as a short sequence of phases. Each phase closes before the next opens, and you can pause at any handover.

1. **Discovery (free).** A free 20-minute call. I learn your team's shape, where AI could help, and what your people are wrestling with today. You get an honest read on whether training is the right move, or whether a harness or an automation would serve you better.
2. **Programme design (paid).** A paid scoping step, because customised means real prep. I learn your goal and what your team actually needs, then design the adoption programme around it, scoped to your workflows, your tools, and the depth you want. This produces the delivery plan and the quote. It is charged, not a freebie, because the design is the work that makes the rest land.
3. **Hands-on training.** The sessions themselves, run on your team's real work rather than toy examples. Mainly Claude Code, plus the general AI grounding your people need to make good calls about which tool fits what.
4. **Supervised practice and a playbook.** Your team practises on their own work with me alongside, and we build up a prompt and playbook library they keep, so the good patterns are written down and repeatable.
5. **Handover.** I step back and the capability stays with your team: the skills, the playbook, and the materials they own. The point is that you do not need me on standby afterwards.

## Who this is for

Teams who want to actually own the capability rather than outsource it. Owner-operators and team leads whose people are smart and busy, sense there is real value in AI, but have not had anyone show them how to use it properly on their own work. If you would rather I just built the thing for you, the harness or automation route fits better, and the discovery call is where we work out which one is right.
